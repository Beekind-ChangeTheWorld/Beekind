New Beehives!

Nov 3, 2023 | The time where La Châtaigneraie welcomed a beehive

After countless meetings involving Henrik and Mr Sues it is finally time for the campus to welcome new pollinators. Initially the beehive was supposed to be situated on the bank of the river flowing through the school, however, this location was highly restrictive because keys had to be deposed each time the hive needed maintenance. Instead another location has luckily been found thanks to the farmer owning the field behind the Lausanne building.

Now that a location has been set the only thing we needed was a beehive with bees in it. The beehive part was quite easy since someone kindly gifted to us. This was a huge help, the beehive was great, after a good polish and a nice paint job bees will be ready to flood in. After underestimating the time taken for two adolescents that have a really rudimentary skill set, the beehive was ready!

Missing in the puzzle was the bees, now you are going to tell me, how do you end up with bees in your beehive? Starting with something called a nuclei which is a beehive with a new queen that has just split up from the previous colony. The nuclei was brought to use by a professional and passionate beekeeper (who kindly gave us a great amount of knowledge about beehives. After having the nuclei, the procedure was easy: we just had to swap the frame of the nuclei’s hive with our hive. After the newly arrived bees will take care of release pheromones that will help all the bees to know where their new home is.
